Working Papers

01

Revisiting Factor Identification in the Chinese Stock Market: A Nonlinear Machine Learning Approach

with Zhuo Wang and Ke Wu · 2026

We provide the first comprehensive nonlinear identification of priced factors in the Chinese stock market by integrating Automatic Debiased Machine Learning (ADML) with a nonlinear stochastic discount factor framework. Using a set of 132 candidate factors, we identify 42 with significant marginal contributions to expected returns: behavioral and sentiment-related factors exhibit strong explanatory power, whereas momentum factors perform weakly. The ADML-identified factor model delivers lower pricing errors and stronger spanning performance than linear double-selection LASSO and widely used benchmark models. We further show that the factor structure changes markedly around the Split-Share Structure Reform and differs systematically by state ownership — providing new evidence that institutional change and ownership heterogeneity jointly shape the factor structure in China's equity market.

Presentations: 2026 China International Conference in Finance (CICF); 2026 CES China Annual Conference; 5th Annual Hong Kong Conference on Fintech and AI in Finance.

02

Anonymization and Information Loss

with Ke Wu, Baozhong Yang, and Dexin Zhou · 2026

Anonymizing financial texts is a widely used technique to reduce look-ahead bias when extracting textual information with large language models. We demonstrate that while anonymization effectively obscures firm identity, it compromises textual understanding and diminishes models' ability to extract meaningful economic signals. This information loss is particularly severe when numerical and agent entities are removed, and is amplified in texts with high linguistic uncertainty, high entity content, or low firm transparency. The effect is pervasive across prediction tasks (firm uncertainty, corporate policy, default), across LLMs, and across text types. Notably, in sentiment extraction from earnings call transcripts, anonymization-induced information loss is more severe than look-ahead bias itself — suggesting that the costs of anonymization can outweigh its benefits in many financial applications.

03

Construction of an Industry Classification Dataset for Chinese A-Share Listed Companies: A Large Language Model Approach

with Ke Wu, Zongxin Qian, and Dexin Zhou · 2026

Existing industry classifications in the Chinese A-share market (CAPCO, Shenwan, Wind) are slow to update and coarse in their treatment of emerging and cross-sector businesses. Using 52,702 Management Discussion & Analysis (MD&A) texts from Chinese A-share annual reports (2007–2023), we combine LLM text embeddings with hierarchical agglomerative clustering to construct a three-tier classification spanning 26 primary, 102 secondary, and 271 tertiary industries. The classification dominates mainstream standards on between-industry heterogeneity and within-industry similarity. Industry-peer hedge portfolios based on our secondary (tertiary) classification deliver statistically significant monthly returns of 1.29% (1.53%) that survive adjustment by the Fama–French five-factor and Chinese four-factor models, providing asset-pricing evidence for the classification's validity.

Data & Software

Public Dataset

Chinese A-Share Industry Classification (LLM-based)

A three-tier industry classification of all Chinese A-share listed companies, constructed by embedding MD&A annual-report texts with a large language model and clustering the resulting semantic vectors. The taxonomy is transparent, reproducible, and can be re-estimated each year — closing a gap in the Chinese literature, where existing classifications are either proprietary or slow to incorporate new business forms. Accompanies the working paper above.
